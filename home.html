Abstract

Yeasts have the potential to be used as microbial cell factories for the production of lipids (biodiesel, high-value lipids, etc.). However, bio-production is costly compared to chemical synthesis, as it is highly energy-consuming for the cell and product extraction is laborious.

To make it more competitive we engineer yeast cells that utilize light both as an inductor for a metabolic switch to start lipid production and as an energy source to drive the lipid synthesis. The cells will be coated by nanoparticles taht absorb light and provide additional electrons for the formation of NADPH, a critical cofactor for lipid synthesis.

This approach allows rapid biomass accumulation before production, followed by light-induced expression of lipid synthesis enzymes resulting in the accumulation of lipid droplets. To ease the extraction of the lipid droplets, we introduce the inducible yeast autolysis method developed last year.
